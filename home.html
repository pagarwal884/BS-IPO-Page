Frequently Asked Questions?

Find answers to common questions that come in your mind related to IPO.

What is an IPO? +
An IPO (Initial Public Offering) is when a private company offers shares to the public for the first time.
Who can apply for an IPO? +
Any Indian citizen with a PAN card and Demat account can apply for IPOs.
What is a price band? +
The price band is the range within which investors can bid for shares in a book-building IPO.
What is a Demat account? +
A Demat account holds your shares in electronic format, similar to how a bank account holds money.
Can I cancel my IPO application? +
Yes, IPO applications can be modified or withdrawn before the IPO closes.
What is the minimum amount to invest in an IPO? +
It depends on the lot size; usually starts from ₹14,000 to ₹15,000 for retail investors.
What happens after I apply for an IPO? +
Funds are blocked in your bank (ASBA) and shares are allotted if selected after the issue closes.
What is the listing date? +
The listing date is the day shares are listed on the stock exchange and start trading.
What is GMP in IPO? +
GMP (Grey Market Premium) is the unofficial premium at which IPO shares are traded before listing.
Is IPO investment risky? +
Yes, IPOs carry market risk. Read RHP/DRHP carefully before investing.
How do I know if I got IPO allotment? +
You can check the allotment status on the registrar's website using your PAN or application number.
What is the difference between DRHP and RHP? +
DRHP is the Draft Red Herring Prospectus filed initially; RHP is the final version with confirmed details.
How is IPO allotment decided? +
Allotment is usually done by lottery or proportionate basis, depending on investor category.
Can I sell IPO shares on listing day? +
Yes, once listed, you can sell IPO shares anytime on the stock exchange.
